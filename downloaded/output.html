# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training Athlete

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to build endurance through carbohydrate-loading while maintaining vegetarian nutrition:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3500 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (130g), Fat (95g), Carbs (490g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), sunflower seeds (30g), and whole milk (250ml).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Whole wheat toast (60g) with peanut butter (40g) and honey (20g). Orange juice (300ml).
    * Protein: 14g, Fat: 16g, Carbs: 65g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Lentil and vegetable curry with chickpeas (200g cooked), brown rice (150g cooked), spinach (100g), tomato (80g), and olive oil (15ml). Side of plain yogurt (100g).
    * Protein: 22g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with hummus (60g), sliced cucumber (100g), and carrot sticks (80g). Apple (180g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 75g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked sweet potato (250g), black bean and quinoa bowl (150g cooked), roasted bell peppers (150g), zucchini (100g), and tahini dressing (20ml).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 110g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** Whole grain crackers (40g) with cheese (50g) and jam (20g). Herbal tea with honey (10g).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 7g, Carbs: 30g

* **Pre-Bed Hydration (Optional):** Coconut water (200ml) for electrolyte replenishment.
    * Protein: 1g, Fat: 0g, Carbs: 10g

This meal plan emphasizes complex carbohydrates and plant-based proteins essential for marathon endurance, while remaining fully vegetarian and nut-free. The carbohydrate-rich distribution supports glycogen storage for sustained energy during training.
